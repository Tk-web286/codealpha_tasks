What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack where attackers impersonate trusted entities to steal sensitive data (passwords, credit cards) or install malware. Below are the main types you must recognize.

Email Phishing
Mass emails with malicious links.
Spear Phishing
Targeted using personal info.
Whaling
CEO / Executives impersonation.
Smishing
SMS text scams.
Vishing: Voice calls pretending to be bank/IT.
QRishing: Malicious QR codes leading to fake portals.

Psychological Triggers

Urgency
"Your account expires in 1 hour! Act now."
Authority
"CEO requires immediate wire transfer."
Greed
"You won $1000 gift card! Claim now."

Real-World Breach Analysis

Attack: Fake O365 login page sent via invoice-themed email. Impact: 100,000+ credentials stolen. Prevention: MFA + conditional access + user awareness.

Method: Spoofed CEO email to finance department requesting urgent payment. Lesson: Always use out-of-band verification for money transfers.

Fake SMS: "Unusual transaction detected – call this number". Victims gave OTP over phone. Prevention: Never share OTP; call bank via official number.
